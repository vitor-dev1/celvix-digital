Content That Converts

Your content is a 24/7 listing presentation. Done right, it sells your expertise before you ever shake a hand.

Posting more isn't the goal — posting with intent is. Buyers and sellers are watching long before they reach out, and the agent whose content feels useful, human, and consistent becomes the obvious call. This guide gives you the content pillars, hook formulas, tour scripts, and a realistic cadence you can actually sustain.

The five content pillars for agents

Rotate these so your feed educates and builds trust instead of just shouting "For Sale":

  • Listings & tours — the properties themselves, shown like a story, not a slideshow.
  • Market education — local data, "is now a good time?", interest-rate context, neighborhood trends.
  • Lifestyle & area — restaurants, schools, commute, the feel of living there. You sell the life, not just the house.
  • Authority & proof — sold stories, testimonials, behind-the-scenes of a deal coming together.
  • Personal & relatable — who you are, why you do this. People hire people, not logos.

Hook formulas for Reels & TikTok

The first two seconds decide everything. If the hook fails, the content never gets seen. Steal these openers:

  1. The bold claim: "This $600k home has one flaw most buyers miss."
  2. The question: "Thinking of selling in [city] this year? Watch this first."
  3. The number: "3 things that instantly drop your home's value."
  4. The contrarian take: "Stop waiting for rates to drop. Here's why."
  5. The reveal: "You won't believe what's behind this door…"
  6. The local angle: "Everyone's moving to [neighborhood]. Here's the real reason."

The property tour script (that holds attention)

A tour isn't a walk-through — it's a narrative with tension and payoff:

  1. Hook (0–2s): Lead with the most striking feature or a bold question. Never start with "Welcome to…".
  2. Stakes (3–8s): Tell them what makes this home special and who it's perfect for.
  3. Tour (8–40s): Move through 3–4 standout moments, pairing each with a benefit, not just a feature ("…so morning coffee here is unreal").
  4. The detail: Show one unexpected detail that makes it memorable and shareable.
  5. Call to action: "Comment 'TOUR' and I'll send you the full walkthrough." Drive a real next step.
Pro Tip

Film in batches. Block two hours, dress in two or three outfits, and shoot a week's worth of hooks and tours at once. Consistency is what the algorithm — and your audience — reward, and batching is the only way busy agents keep it up. A posting cadence you can sustain

Consistency beats volume. A realistic, high-impact week looks like:

  • 3–5 short-form videos (Reels/TikTok/Shorts) across your pillars.
  • 2–3 stories per day — low effort, high trust: behind the scenes, polls, day-in-the-life.
  • 1 educational or "sold" post per week to anchor your authority.
  • Engage 15 minutes daily — reply to every comment and DM fast. Reach follows engagement.

Repurpose everything (work once, post ten times)

One good idea should never be a single post. Turn each one into a system:

  • One property tour → a Reel, a carousel of stills, three story frames, and a YouTube Short.
  • One market stat → a talking-head Reel, a graphic post, and an email to your sphere.
  • One testimonial → a quote graphic, a video clip, and a "client win" story.
  • Caption your videos — most are watched on mute.
  • Repost your best performer every 60–90 days; new followers never saw it.
