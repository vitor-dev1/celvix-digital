The Real Estate Lead Engine

Leads are not luck. They're the output of a system you can build, measure, and scale — predictably, month after month.

Most agents ride a feast-or-famine cycle because their pipeline depends on referrals and timing. The agents who grow steadily treat lead generation like an engine: a defined funnel, consistent inputs, fast follow-up, and metrics they actually watch. This playbook lays out the full system — paid and organic — plus the benchmarks to know whether it's working.

Paid vs. organic: use both, on purpose

They solve different problems. The winning approach runs them together.

  • Paid (Meta & Google ads, listing boosts) — fast, scalable, and controllable. You can turn volume up or down this week. The cost is real money per lead and the need for sharp creative and targeting.
  • Organic (content, referrals, sphere, local SEO) — slower to build but compounding. It lowers your blended cost per lead over time and produces warmer, higher-trust prospects.

Rule of thumb: use paid to control volume this month, and organic to lower your cost and warm your audience next quarter. Skipping either leaves money on the table.

Build the funnel (TOFU → MOFU → BOFU)

  1. Top of funnel — attract. Reels, market updates, home-value ads, neighborhood guides. Goal: reach and a captured contact (a "What's my home worth?" form or a saved follow).
  2. Middle of funnel — nurture. Email and DM sequences, retargeting, helpful content. Goal: build trust and identify who's actually moving.
  3. Bottom of funnel — convert. Consultations, showings, listing appointments. Goal: a booked meeting and a signed agreement.

Every lead source should map to a stage. If you're generating top-of-funnel attention with no nurture in the middle, you're paying to fill a leaky bucket.

Qualify leads fast (don't chase everyone)

Time is your scarcest asset. Qualify against four quick signals:

  • Motivation — why are they moving, and how real is it?
  • Timeline — buying/selling in 30 days, 90 days, or "someday"?
  • Financial readiness — pre-approved, cash, or still exploring?
  • Decision-making — are you talking to everyone who has a say?

Score each lead A/B/C. A-leads get a call within minutes; B-leads enter nurture; C-leads get long-term drip. Protect your hours for the prospects who can actually transact.

The follow-up cadence that converts

Speed and persistence beat polish. Studies consistently show contact rates fall off a cliff after the first five minutes, yet most agents quit after one or two attempts. Use a structured cadence:

  1. Minute 1–5: Call immediately. If no answer, text and email right away.
  2. Day 1: Second call + a value-first message (a relevant listing or market stat).
  3. Day 2–3: Third touch across a different channel — DM or video message.
  4. Day 5–7: Fourth touch with a soft call to action ("Want me to send three options?").
  5. Day 10+: Move non-responders into a weekly long-term nurture. Don't delete — recycle.
Pro Tip

Record a 20-second personalized video for every A-lead in your first follow-up. A face, a name, and one specific detail about their search lifts reply rates dramatically over a plain text. Batch them — film five in ten minutes. Bring your current follow-up scripts to a strategy call and we'll tighten them and build the video templates with you.

Cost-per-lead benchmarks (so you know what "good" looks like)

Numbers vary by market and price point, but these ranges give you a sanity check:

  • Buyer leads (paid social): roughly $8–$40 per lead depending on competition and offer.
  • Seller / home-valuation leads: typically $20–$80+ — higher cost, but far higher value.
  • Lead-to-appointment rate: aim for 5–10% of raw online leads booking a real conversation.
  • Cost per closing: the metric that matters most — track total ad spend ÷ deals closed, not just cost per lead.

A "cheap" lead that never closes is expensive. Always trace the number all the way down to closings.

Your lead engine checklist

  • One active paid campaign (buyer or seller offer) with sharp creative and a clear hook.
  • A nurture sequence (email + DM) running automatically for new leads.
  • A documented 5-touch follow-up cadence your whole process follows.
  • An A/B/C scoring habit so your time goes to ready buyers and sellers.
  • A simple dashboard: leads, appointments, cost per lead, cost per closing.
  • A monthly review to cut what's underperforming and scale what's working.
